Background

The SOSO mobile website was first built in 2011. As SOSO search engine included several different subtypes-- such as SOSO music, SOSO Map, SOSO pictures, SOSO news, etc. , and since there were not enough designers to support this program, it took some months to gradually complete the launch of mobile web pages of them. In this case, the design was not identical among these subsidiary products, thus the mobile web design did not provide a good-quality user experience. Meanwhile, the former mobile web design of SOSO was outmoded to some extent, which could not perfectly meet the needs of increasing product features. Therefore, an overhaul of SOSO mobile web was just around the corner.

Keywords

As one of the key points of search engine is to find the answers efficiently, we decided to keep the design “simple” and “light” to express the fastness of SOSO. Moreover, as we knew from the market research that the target users of SOSO were young people between 16 to 30 years old, “young” should be another keyword of the redesign.

Current Problems

Drafts

As the interaction designer tried to solve the existing problems with a improved layout, I was asked to build a better image through graphic design to impress users that SOSO search engine is light and handy. After the layout of the website was settled, I drawn up some design drafts for the product manager and the team to choose. Since the interaction was identical, the difference between these drafts were the details, such as the model of the search bar, the icon color and style, and so forth.

New Strategy

Functional Partition

To reasonably use space, the homepage was divided into four areas—logo and search bar, entrances of subsets, personal information (sign in/ sign up), supplementary information (entrance of other version, and copyright).

Color

To make the page seems lighter, I changed the search bar from blue into white, as well as designed icons by the brand color of SOSO—blue instead of using too much colors.

Visual Style

Besides adopting simple shape icon style, I increased the radian of arc of icons to make them more adorable to young people. Moreover, decrease of using lines to divide zones enabled the webpage light and airy.

Final Version of SOSO Web Search

After two times of meeting and discussion regarding all the drafts, the final design was decided. During the following days, the main pages were designed (both Day mode and Night mode) and confirmed by the main designers and senior project managers.

Guidance of Visual Design

Since there were several subsidiary products of SOSO—which means plenty of web pages-- had to be revised according to the new visual style, to ensure the consistency of design among these sub-products, I summarized a set of guidance about all the widgets and supervised the redesign work implemented by different designers.
